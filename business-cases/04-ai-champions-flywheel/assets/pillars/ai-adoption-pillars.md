# AI Champions Pillars — Detailed Design  

This document details the **four core pillars** that structure the AI Champions Flywheel and enable the transition from experimentation to an embedded AI capability across the organization.  

The model translates AI adoption into a structured operating system, combining **behavioral change, capability building and governance mechanisms**. Each pillar defines how AI is activated, scaled and sustained in daily business operations.  

Rather than a one-off rollout, this approach distributes ownership across the organization, ensuring that AI becomes a shared capability rather than a centralized initiative.

<details>
<summary><b>01. Role Modelling — Champions Network 👥</b></summary>

### Purpose  
Establish a network of AI Champions who act as **role models**, translating AI principles into behaviors and driving adoption within their domains.  

---

### Who are the AI Champions  

- Leaders and influential profiles acting as reference points within the organization  
- Distributed across business domains, functions and geographies  
- Cover different seniorities and roles (from executives to operational teams)  
- Selected based on credibility, proximity to business and engagement  

---

### Mission  

AI Champions operate across three key dimensions:  

- **AI is your Business**  
  Engage teams to understand AI’s value and integrate it into daily activities  

- **Voice the AI**  
  Promote AI as a critical business asset through communication and visibility  

- **Act on AI**  
  Translate between business and technology to drive AI usage in decision-making  

---

### Key Mechanisms  

- Initial **kick-off to align roles**, expectations and behaviors  
- Active participation in **AI programs**: sessions, workshops, among other initiatives  
- Symbolic and visible actions reinforcing AI adoption  
- Translation layer between business needs and AI capabilities  
- Promotion of transparency and escalation on AI-related topics  

---

### Actions  

- Promote AI usage within teams and domains  
- Identify and activate **high-impact use cases**  
- Reinforce AI principles and best practices  
- Facilitate collaboration across teams  
- Support adoption of approved tools and solutions  

---

### Tools

- **Microsoft Teams**: *AI Champions Community*
  → Dedicated channel for champions coordination, peer exchange and visibility
- **Viva Engage**: Yammer
  → Storytelling layer for sharing wins, use cases and recognition across the organization
- **SharePoint**: Community Hub
  → Centralized space for champions directory, materials and initiatives
- **Microsoft Loop**
  → Lightweight collaboration for co-creating ideas, use cases and shared think
  → [AI Use Case Template](../../assets/templates/ai-use-case-template.md)

---

### Outcomes  

- Increased proximity between AI initiatives and business needs  
- Faster activation of use cases  
- Stronger peer-driven adoption  
- Foundation for a scalable AI Champions community  

---

### Evolution  

From individual champions to a connected community:  

- Local influence → Collective intelligence  
- Isolated actions → Shared learning loops  
- Program participation → Ownership of AI adoption  

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>02. Awareness — AI Democratization 📣</b></summary>

### Purpose  
Enhance understanding of AI as a critical enabler of business performance and drive organization-wide engagement.  

---

### Key Mechanisms  

- Activation of internal communication channels (Teams, SharePoint, intranet)  
- Leadership endorsement through townhalls and internal events  
- AI-focused campaigns and storytelling initiatives  
- Community-driven content and knowledge sharing  

---

### Core Themes  

- AI as a driver of business outcomes and decision-making  
- Importance of responsible and governed AI usage  
- Consequences of poor data/AI practices vs benefits of adoption  
- Real use cases and success stories  

---

### Actions  

- Launch awareness campaigns across internal channels  
- Share use cases, demos and quick wins  
- Promote AI narratives through leadership and champions  
- Encourage participation in events, sessions and forums  

---

### Tools

- **SharePoint**: AI Hub / Knowledge Center
  → Repository of AI use cases, guidelines and best practices
- **Viva Engage** + **Teams Announcements**
  → Campaign distribution, internal storytelling and engagement
- **Power BI**
  → Visualizing impact of AI use cases (adoption, efficiency, outcomes)
- **Microsoft Stream / Clipchamp**
  → Short-form video content to explain AI concepts and use cases

---

### Outcomes  

- Increased visibility of AI across the organization  
- Improved understanding of AI value  
- Reduction of resistance and misconceptions  
- Higher engagement in AI initiatives  

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>03. Capability Building — Upskilling 🎓</b></summary>

### Purpose  
Build the skills and capabilities required to enable effective and responsible AI usage across roles.  

---

### Training Structure  

- **Baseline training (mandatory)**  
  AI fundamentals, usage principles and governance  

- **Role-based training**  
  Tailored learning paths aligned with business functions  

- **Hands-on learning**  
  Workshops, simulations and real use case scenarios  

---

### Key Mechanisms  

- Structured learning programs integrated into onboarding  
- Practical workshops and role-play sessions  
- Office hours and continuous support  
- Training linked to real business use cases  

---

### Actions  

- Deliver training sessions (baseline and advanced)  
- Enable experimentation through practical scenarios  
- Support continuous learning and capability development  
- Share reusable knowledge and best practices  

---

### Tools

- **Microsoft Learn / Viva Learning**
  → Structured learning paths tailored to roles
- **Azure Machine Learning / Azure OpenAI**
  → Hands-on exposure to real AI capabilities
- **Databricks** (Azure integration)
  → Advanced analytics and model experimentation environment
- **GitHub** (this portfolio included 😉)
  → Sharing code, templates and structured use cases

---

### Outcomes  

- Increased AI literacy across teams  
- Faster onboarding into AI usage  
- Higher quality and relevance of use cases  
- Reduced dependency on specialized teams  

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>04. AI Discipline — Governance & Risk 🛡️</b></summary>

### Purpose  
Ensure safe, responsible and scalable AI adoption through governance frameworks and risk management.  

---

### Key Mechanisms  

- AI risk assessment aligned with EU AI Act  
- Definition of AI usage principles and guidelines  
- Monitoring and escalation processes  
- Integration with data governance structures  

---

### Governance Components  

- Risk classification of AI use cases  
- Approval and validation processes  
- Incident reporting and tracking  
- Compliance with internal and regulatory requirements  

---

### Actions  

- Assess AI use cases based on risk and impact  
- Enforce governance standards and policies  
- Monitor AI usage and detect risks  
- Promote usage of approved tools and models  

---

### Tools

- **Microsoft Purview**
  → Data governance, cataloging, lineage and policy enforcement
- **Azure AI Studio / Model Registry**
  → Model lifecycle management, versioning and traceability
- **Power Platform** (Power Apps + Power Automate)
  → Use case intake forms (AI Champions submissions)
  → Workflow automation for validation and approval
- **Oxethica**
  → AI risk assessment aligned with EU AI Act (certified capability)
- **ServiceNow**
  → Incident management, escalation and compliance tracking

--- 

### Outcomes  

- Increased trust in AI systems  
- Reduced risk and misuse  
- Compliance with regulatory frameworks  
- Sustainable and scalable adoption  

</details>

## System View — How the Pillars Work Together  

The four pillars operate as an integrated system that continuously reinforces AI adoption:  

- **Awareness** creates understanding and engagement  
- **Capability Building** enables practical usage  
- **Role Modelling** drives behavioral change  
- **AI Discipline** ensures trust, safety and scalability  

Together, they form a self-reinforcing flywheel where:  

→ Adoption drives usage  
→ Usage generates value  
→ Value reinforces engagement  
→ Governance sustains growth  

AI evolves from a tool into an embedded organizational capability.
